Mahatma Gandhi

This image captures the essence of the leader who spearheaded India's freedom movement through passive resistance and civil disobedience

    Timelines:-

  1. Oct 2, 1869 – Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, into a devout Hindu family
  2. April 1893 – Heads to South Africa to work as a lawyer; is forcibly removed from a train, igniting his activism
  3. 1894 – Founds the Natal Indian Congress to defend Indian rights
  4. 1906 – Develops the concept of satyagraha (nonviolent resistance) amid protests against discriminatory laws
  5. 1915 – Returns to India and begins work on social reform and independence campaigns
  6. 1917 – Leads first Indian satyagraha in Champaran, Bihar, defending tenant farmers
  7. 1920–22 – Heads the Non-Cooperation Movement; jailed in 1922
  8. Mar–Apr 1930 – Leads the renowned Salt March to Dandi, defying the British salt tax
  9. Aug 1942–44 – Launches the Quit India Movement; imprisoned under emergency powers
  10. Aug 15, 1947 – Witnesses India's independence and partition; fasts to restore Hindu–Muslim unity amid violence
  11. Jan 30, 1948 – Tragically assassinated in New Delhi by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist
